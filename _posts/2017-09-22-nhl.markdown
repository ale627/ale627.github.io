I got the last minute invite to join Brian with an extra ticket to the suites at the Sharks preseason NHL game last night.  His cousin Eric works at MicroSemi, a semiconductor company.  MicroSemi has a suite at SAP Center.

We joined a gathering of NVIDIA and MicroSemi folks who work together.  San Jose Sharks vs the brand spankin' new Golden Knights from Las Vegas.

*When a new team like the Golden Knights is chartered - one MicroSemi guy explained - instead of only being able to hire from the free agent pool, each existing team reserves a certain number of players, then the new team selects some unreserved players from each team for their roster.*  

Catering provided beer and wine, hotdogs, sliders, and cookies.  Of course nothing is free and everything is overpriced but MicroSemi set us up well.  The suites, which run around the whole stadium, are all in the nosebleed section, but you can see the puck at all times due to the angle of the seating and the rink lighting.

The game was exciting with 7 goals total.  No scores in the first period, with 4 in a row by San Jose in the 2nd.  At this point I admit to betting Brian $5 on the Knights scoring.  

Final score San Jose 5 - Las Vegas 2.  
Good fun!  Thanks again to Brian, Eric and MicroSemi.
