I got the last minute invite to join Brian with his extra ticket at the Sharks preseason NHL game last night.  His cousin Eric worked at MicroSemi, a semiconductor company which had a suite at SAP Center.

It was a gathering of Nvidia and MicroSemi folks who work together, and then us.  San Jose Sharks vs the brand spankin' new Golden Knights from Las Vegas.  Apparently, instead of being composed of only free agents, what happens when a new team is chartered, one guy explained, is each team reserves a certain number of players, then the new team goes around and selects a couple unreserved players from each team to form the new team.  

There was catered beer and wine, hotdogs, sliders, and cookies.  The suites, which run around the whole stadium, are basically in the nosebleed section, but due to the angle of the seating and the rink lighting, you can see the puck at all times.  

The game was exciting with 7 goals total.  No scores in the first period, with 4 in a row by San Jose in the 2nd.  At this point I admit to betting Brian $5 on the Knights scoring.  Final score San Jose 5 - Las Vegas 2.  
Good fun!  Thanks again to Brian's cousin Eric and MicroSemi for having us.
